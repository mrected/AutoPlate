Spot color printing requirements

Art must be in a vector format. Vector format means your art was created in a vector program and saved in a file that saves the vector information, such as .ai, .pdf or .eps for example. Raster images, such jpeg, png, tiff, psd, etc, can be used but must be at least 200dpi, sent to the correct size, and can only be substituted for black images.

Art that does not meet these requirements may incur an art charge.

Placing a raster image in a vector file does not automatically convert the image to vector format.

For more information on this see our digital art specifications.

Vector Art

Vector art is art created in a vector based program such as Adobe Illustrator© or Corel Draw© and is comprised of vector based shapes. Vector files containing placed raster images (.jpg, .tif, .gif, .png, etc.) do not qualify as acceptable vector art and may incur an art charge.

For more information on this see our digital art specifications.
